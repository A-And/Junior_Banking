When you earn money you should think to put this cash in a safe place! You don’t need to try hiding or storing it as it might result with losing it as you might forget where you’ve put it when hiding too well. The safe place is a bank, where you can keep your money safe when you not intend to spend it all straight away.

Banks give you more options such as saving account where you can put money there and then banks give you a little more money, for keeping it there, which is called interest.

Another option is checking accounts, your parents are probably paying for thing like bills, electricity or rent using checks, no cash! If you have account in a bank, you can easy manage you money by using phone or ATMs, you don’t have to go a bank to get some money!

You can buy very expensive things even you don’t have enough money because the bank help you by lending you the money, it is called loans and credit cards. Remember that you will need to return at some point that loans with interest as bank pays you interest for them to keep money.

Banks are everywhere and the money comes to bank from treasury. You have some money in cash or check and you don’t want to lose it, so you can put your money into a bank and deposit it into your savings account. It is interest because a bank pay you and you are earning the money!

The idea is that money move in sort of circle, from one person to another. When you withdraw money, then buy something then this money you left in the store will be given to employee as wage, or spend in different way.

A bank is a place that looks after people’s money for them and keeps it safe. It also lends money to people to help them buy things, such as houses, cars or bikes.

A budget is a plan on how you will spend the money that you earn and save up. It includes the amount of money that you earn, details on what you want to spend that money on and how much you will need to save.

Saving means not spending the money that you earn straight away. Normally people put their money in a bank account until they have saved enough to buy what they want, using their budget.

A bank account is a record that your bank keeps to know how much money you have given them to look after. You are able to put money in your bank account and withdraw it and you are given a unique account number to identify your bank account.

An account number is a unique number that identifies your account in the banks records

A sort-code is a six digit code that identifies what bank and which branch your account is at. You normally use this to tell other people where your bank account is.

A deposit is when you put money in your bank account

A withdrawal is when you take money out of your bank account.

A withdrawal is when you take money out of your bank account.

Interest is money paid into your account by the bank at the end of each financial year (normally at the start of April), which is usually a percentage of what is currently in your account, as a reward for saving your money.

A Current Account is a regular bank account, which you can use to pay for things or receive money through.

An Independent Savings Account (Or ISA) is an account independent from your current account designed for saving money via interest.

Get a savings account

  • If you really want to save a lot of money then getting a savings account is important and it should be separate to your normal account
  • With Lloyds’ Junior ISA, you will be paid 3% on top of everything you save each year!

Start saving your money now

  • The next step is to start putting money in your account, starting with whatever money you have, even if it is a small amount, it all helps.
  • The best thing to do is make a budget or saving plan and decide how much you’re willing to save every time you earn some money and then do it.

Investing your money

  • If you have a really serious goal then the best idea is to start making your money work for you.
  • The way you do this is by investing your money somewhere it’s going to make more interest than in your savings account.
